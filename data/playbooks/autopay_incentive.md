# Auto-Pay Enrolment Incentive

## Churn Signal
Customer pays by electronic check rather than credit/debit card autopay or bank-transfer
autopay. Manual payment methods correlate with higher churn: they require monthly re-engagement
with the bill and create "pain-of-paying" friction that prompts customers to re-evaluate
their subscription.

## Retention Tactics

- **Autopay discount**: Offer a £2–3 per month discount for customers who switch from
  electronic check to card or direct-debit autopay. The discount more than pays for itself
  through reduced churn and lower payment-processing costs. Communicate the offer as a
  convenience benefit, not just a discount.

- **One-click enrolment in the customer portal**: Reduce the friction of switching by placing
  an "Enrol in Autopay and save £X/month" card at the top of the billing page for eligible
  customers. Lowering the click-count from 5 to 2 increases conversion significantly.

- **Payment reminder reframe**: For customers who stay on electronic check, reframe the
  monthly payment reminder email as a "bill summary" with a clear CTA to switch to autopay.
  Include a testimonial or social-proof element ("X customers saved money this month by
  switching to autopay") to reduce activation energy.
