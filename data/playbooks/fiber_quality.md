# Fiber-Optic Service Quality Management

## Churn Signal
Customer subscribes to fiber-optic internet service. Fiber optic customers churn at higher
rates than DSL customers in the Telco dataset, likely reflecting speed-expectation gaps,
installation friction, or premium pricing resentment when perceived quality is low.

## Retention Tactics

- **Proactive speed and quality check**: Trigger an automated speed-test invitation 30 days
  after fiber installation and again at 90 days. Customers who report speeds below 80 % of
  their promised tier should receive a technician callback within 48 hours. Resolving quality
  issues before they fester reduces churn by ~20 % in the first year.

- **Fibre upgrade path communication**: For customers already on the lowest fiber tier, send
  a comparison card showing their current speeds versus the next tier (with a time-limited
  upgrade offer). Positioning fiber as a journey, not a commodity, increases commitment.

- **Reliability SLA credit**: Introduce an automatic bill credit for any month where measured
  uptime falls below 99.5 %. Proactively crediting accounts — before the customer complains —
  is significantly more effective at reducing churn than reactive compensation.
