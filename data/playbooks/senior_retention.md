# Senior Customer and Household Retention

## Churn Signal
Customer is a senior citizen (SeniorCitizen = 1) and/or has no Partner or Dependents.
Single-household and senior customers have lower perceived switching cost, may have less
familiarity with self-service tools, and respond better to human-touch interactions than
digital-only outreach.

## Retention Tactics

- **Dedicated agent assignment**: Assign a named account manager or preferred-agent
  programme for customers who are 65+ or consistently call for account queries. Relationship
  continuity (calling and reaching the same person) is the single highest-impact lever for
  this segment, reducing churn by up to 30 % versus the general population.

- **Simplified plan review call**: Schedule an annual "plan health check" phone call for
  senior customers, framed as a service, not a sales call. The agent reviews usage, confirms
  the plan still fits, and makes any adjustments. This touchpoint surfaces dissatisfaction
  before it becomes a cancellation.

- **Household or caregiver referral programme**: Offer a modest bill credit to seniors who
  refer a family member or caregiver to join the service. Household bundling (multiple lines
  or accounts under one billing relationship) is the strongest single predictor of long-term
  retention in this segment.
